Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

Having an MRI Scan

This page provides information about MRI scans. It is intended for use by patients (or their families and carers) who have been referred to our service.

What is an MRI scan?

An MRI scan is an examination used to diagnose medical conditions, injuries, or diseases. Once the scan is complete, a radiologist analyzes the images and sends a report to your doctor.

The scanner works by using a strong magnetic field and radio waves to interact with the hydrogen atoms naturally found in your body.

How can MRI help?
MRI is used to visualise and distinguish selected areas of the body from surrounding tissues. MRI is particularly good for looking at muscles and soft tissue structures.

Before your MRI scan

Important: Please contact radiology prior to your scan if you:
  • Require hospital transport, hoist assistance or use a wheelchair
  • Require an interpreter
  • Are currently pregnant

You must tell us if you have ever had any of the following implanted:

  • Cardiac Pacemaker or cardiac device
  • Coronary (heart) stents
  • Artificial Heart Valve
  • Cochlear implant
  • Any operations in the last six weeks involving metallic implants

You may have been sent a Patient Screening form with your appointment letter.

Download MRI Patient Screening Form (PDF)

Medication and Eating Guidance

Unless you have been directed otherwise you should eat or drink normally before and after your scan.

Specific Exceptions:
  • MMRCP (Gall bladder): Fast for 4 hours prior.
  • Cardiac Stress Perfusion: No caffeine for 25 hours prior.

What to wear

You can wear:
  • T-shirts, Leggings, Pyjamas
  • Tracksuits (metal free)
You should NOT wear:
  • Bras (unless metal-free)
  • Jeans, Belts, Zips

Asking for your consent

We want you to be fully involved in decisions about your care. Before we carry out the scan, we are required by law to ask for your consent.

What are the risks?

As with any medical procedure, there are some risks involved.

Pregnancy

If you are pregnant, please contact Radiology. We take extra care during pregnancy.

Download Pregnancy and MRI Info (PIL5156)

During the scan

You will be asked to lie flat on the scanner table. The radiographer will help you place single-use earplugs.

  • Loud Noise: Tapping and banging noises.
  • Sensations: Occasional twitching.

After your scan

Once your scan is complete you will be free to go home. A radiologist will review your scan.

Alternatives and Choice

Alternatives such as CT or ultrasound may exist but aren't always suitable for every condition.
